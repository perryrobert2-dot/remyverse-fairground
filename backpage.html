Arthur Pumble's Mailbag

*** **THE REMY DIGEST - Letters to the Editor** ## The Spit Gripes **By Arthur Pumble, Concerned Citizen** Good heavens, another week has spun its dizzying web, and with it, another deluge of anxieties, gripes, and outright philosophical quandaries from the discerning readership of 'The Remy Digest'. One might think, scanning the daily effluent of global catastrophe, that the small man’s worries would shrink to insignificance. Nonsense! The smaller the worry, the sharper the barb, the deeper the wound. And it is in these precise, exquisitely painful naggings that the true character of our age is revealed. Welcome, then, to another edition of that most necessary of public services, where your personal indignations find their rightful place in the annals of absurdity. --- **THE SHADOW OF THE LAUREL** Dear Mr. Pumble, I feel compelled to write to your esteemed column, as I find myself at the end of my tether, and indeed, at the end of my carefully cultivated Privet hedge. For years, I have maintained a meticulous border between my property and that of my next-door neighbours, the Millers. A simple matter of respect, one would think, and a cornerstone of civilised cohabitation. However, in recent months, I have observed, with increasing dismay, that Mr. Miller’s 'ornamental' shrubbery – a particularly aggressive species of laurel, I believe – has taken it upon itself to embark upon a veritable annexation of my airspace! Not content with merely overhanging, I now find its tendrils practically tickling my kitchen window, shedding its rather noxious leaves onto my freshly swept patio, and, dare I say it, casting an altogether oppressive shadow upon my prize-winning Fuchsias. I have attempted subtle hints – a well-placed ladder, a pair of secateurs left pointedly on the dividing wall – but to no avail. One wonders if common decency has entirely withered, much like my poor petunias beneath Mr. Miller’s encroaching foliage. A man’s home, Mr. Pumble, is his castle, not a shared botanical free-for-all. I trust you will air this most pressing of local indignities. Yours in exasperation, **Outraged of Cromer.** --- *Ah, the domestic frontier. More vicious than any geopolitical squabble, I assure you. The humble shrub, once a symbol of nature's gentle embrace, now a weapon in the silent war of suburban wills. But fear not, Outraged, for the seeds of your complaint have clearly been heard across the garden fence...* --- **A CERTAIN GREEN VIBRANCY** Dear Mr. Pumble, It was with a peculiar mix of bemusement and, dare I say, a touch of sadness, that I read the letter from 'Outraged of Cromer' in last week's esteemed 'Digest'. One does rather feel for those who find such distress in the simple ebb and flow of nature, particularly in a community so dedicated to greenery as ours. While I wouldn't dream of commenting on specific hedges or indeed, on the horticultural choices of others, I must confess that our own little laurel, which we find so wonderfully robust and indeed, rather attractive to the local birdlife, has been thriving since we nourished it with only the finest organic compost. It brings such a delightful verdant touch to the area, don't you think? And as for 'tickling' kitchen windows or 'shedding' leaves, well, one simply must embrace the seasons, mustn't one? Perhaps a good sweeping brush is simply part of the joyful rhythm of homeownership, rather than a harbinger of botanical warfare. Indeed, we've often thought our little patch of green provides a most welcome splash of natural vibrancy against the somewhat… starker… landscaping of certain adjacent properties. Perhaps a more embracing view of the natural world, rather than a confrontational one, might bring a little more peace to some? It’s always a delight to see the varied ways our neighbours choose to express themselves, even through the medium of the local press. We shall continue to enjoy our garden, as we hope everyone else does theirs. Warmest regards (and a perfectly delightful cup of herbal tea awaits, should anyone wish to discuss gardening *sensibly*), **A Neighbourly Soul, Cromer.** --- There you have it. The suburban jungle, as rich in intrigue and unspoken menace as any rainforest. One man's prize fuchsia, another's 'stark landscaping'. One person's grievance, another's offer of *sensible* tea. Truly, the human spirit, when confined by property lines and petty grievances, blossoms into the most magnificent, venomous flower. Keep those grievances coming, dear readers. For in your indignation, I find my own bleak solace. Until next week, try not to trip over the truth. ***

Madame Fifi's Seance

The Seance: Madame Fifi Channels the Dead

### **SEANCE by Madame Fifi** *For The Remy Digest* Dearest readers, or should I say, *fellow travelers* on this rather peculiar journey we call existence? Madame Fifi here, paws firmly planted on the astral plane (and occasionally in a particularly delectable puddle), ready once more to peel back the veil of the mundane. My bespoke crystal ball, currently suffering a minor structural integrity issue thanks to a particularly enthusiastic squirrel-chase, is temporarily out of commission. Worry not, for my antennae, finely tuned by years of discerning sniffing and an insatiable curiosity for the Great Beyond (and an even greater one for leftover roast chicken), remain fully operational. One might question the utility of communicating with the departed, especially when the living are so confoundingly dull. Yet, there is a certain *je ne sais quoi* to eavesdropping on eternity. This week, eschewing the usual pleas for lottery numbers or the whereabouts of Aunt Mildred’s misplaced dentures, I decided to reach for a truly singular spirit. A pioneer, they called her. A cosmic trailblazer. Yes, my dears, we’re communing with the one, the only, **Laika**. *A brief tremor ripples through the chaise lounge. Madame Fifi's whiskers twitch, her elegant snout pointed at a non-existent point in the ceiling, as a faint, distant bark echoes in the room.* *** "So, space. You'd think it'd be full of interesting new smells. Turns out, it's mostly... nothing. A vast, echoing void of olfactory disappointment. One really begins to ponder the true meaning of 'zero-g' when your kibble crumbs are perpetually orbiting your nose, just out of reach, a cosmic game of 'fetch' without the fetch. Down on Earth, there was that delightful patch of particularly pungent hydrant, a masterpiece of canine cartography. Up here? Just recycled air, faintly redolent of stale ambition and the faint metallic tang of regret. Honestly, a good belly rub would have been far more scientifically significant." *** There you have it. The cosmic truth, straight from the… well, the dog's mouth, I suppose. And to think, humanity sent her up there to "advance science," when all she really wanted was a decent sniff and some gravity for her dinner. Typical. One would think, given the vastness of the universe, there’d be better amenities. Until next time, keep your spirits up, and your treat jar full. *Avec a knowing wink and a well-placed paw-print,* **Madame Fifi** *Standard Poodle, Extraordinary Medium, Discerning Critic of Celestial Cuisine*
