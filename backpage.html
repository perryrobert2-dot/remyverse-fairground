Arthur Pumble's Mailbag

## The Spit Gripes Welcome, dear readers, to 'The Spit Gripes' – that hallowed (or perhaps merely hollowed) corner of The Remy Digest where the collective sigh of a nation finds its print. I am Arthur Pumble, your humble, or rather, increasingly humbug-ridden, custodian of complaints. Another week, another deluge of... well, gripes. One wonders if the human condition is merely a perpetual state of mild irritation, punctuated only by tea breaks and the occasional seismic shift in local council policy regarding wheelie bin collections. Still, better out than in, eh? Like a particularly obstinate splinter, these minor indignities fester until committed to newsprint. Let us delve, shall we, into the festering wounds of the common man, and indeed, the uncommon woman, who dares to put pen to paper (or, more likely, finger to keyboard, *sigh*). --- **THE RHODODENDRON OF DOOM** Dear Mr. Pumble, I write to you today not merely as a concerned citizen, but as a man pushed to the very brink of civility. For too long, the 'natural beauty' – as some might so quaintly put it – of my neighbour's rhododendrons has encroached upon my prize-winning petunias. But that, sir, is merely the tip of the iceberg, or should I say, the top of the overzealous shrub! Last Tuesday, at precisely 7:17 AM – a time I had previously reserved for the quiet contemplation of my morning bran flakes – I was assailed by the infernal cacophony of a petrol hedge trimmer. A noise, I might add, that could wake the dead, or at the very least, severely disorient a man attempting to enjoy the dawn chorus (which, by the way, was entirely drowned out). Not only did this mechanical monstrosity shatter the peace, but the detritus from the trimming – twigs, leaves, and what appeared to be half a robin's nest – was left to drift wantonly across our shared boundary. A boundary, I hasten to remind you, that *I* maintain to an impeccable standard! Where is the consideration? Where is the common decency? Are we, in this so-called modern age, to simply tolerate such blatant disregard for a neighbour's right to quiet enjoyment and an untarnished lawn? I fear for the future, Mr. Pumble, I truly do. Yours, in perpetual exasperation, *Outraged of Cromer* --- **A BREATH OF FRESH, IF NOISY, AIR** Dear Mr. Pumble, I feel compelled to respond to certain... observations regarding garden maintenance in the Cromer vicinity. While I commend anyone's passion for petunias, one must surely acknowledge that nature, in its infinite wisdom, occasionally requires a firm hand. And a loud one, if the tools available are of a robust and efficient calibre. Indeed, it was with the utmost consideration for *all* local flora – and fauna, I might add, given the recent surge in bird activity – that I undertook the necessary task of taming an admittedly exuberant rhododendron last Tuesday. One finds, alas, that leaving nature entirely to its own devices results in a rather unkempt vista, which I personally find far more distressing than a brief burst of industrious sound. As for 'detritus', I believe the term 'natural mulch' might be more appropriate. A small scattering of organic matter can only enrich the soil, surely? Though I confess I did not meticulously sweep every leaf from a neighbouring lawn – a task which, I imagine, one might find rather invigorating should one choose to undertake it oneself. Perhaps some individuals simply prefer their gardens to be... sterile? For my part, I find life, even in its louder manifestations, to be a rather glorious thing. With sincerest wishes for a bountiful growing season, *A Neighbour Who Appreciates Vim & Vigour, Cromer* --- Ah, the timeless dance of the green-fingered gladiators! One man's necessary pruning is another's sonic assault and botanical invasion. It rather reminds me of the great Compost Bin Controversy of '97, which, tragically, ended in a rather unsightly incident involving a rogue marrow and a very confused terrier. But I digress. Such is the modern condition, my friends: a constant low hum of aggrieved sensibility. Do not hesitate to add your own particular hum to the cacophony. Send your woes, your rants, your perfectly reasonable yet utterly ignored proclamations of injustice to me, Arthur Pumble, at The Remy Digest. No complaint too small, no outrage too petty. Until next week, keep your hedges trimmed and your tempers... well, keep them simmering, at least. It makes for better reading. Yours in (grudging) solidarity, Arthur Pumble, Concerned Citizen (and occasional earplug wearer).

Madame Fifi's Seance

The Seance: Madame Fifi Channels the Dead

# SEANCE **By Madame Fifi, Resident Canine Clairvoyant** Greetings, denizens of the corporeal plane, and welcome once more to my weekly communion with the Great Beyond – or, as I like to call it, the Celestial Biscuit Tin. One would think that after centuries of human folly, the departed would have more profound insights to offer, but alas, most merely grumble about the lack of decent naps and the eternal search for a squeaky toy in the ether. *Hmph.* Some things, it seems, never change, even when you’re floating amongst the cosmic dust bunnies. This week, my usually impeccably coiffed antennae quivered with a peculiar sensation. Not the usual aroma of stale regret and unmet deadlines that permeates most human existences, but rather a faint, metallic tang mixed with the distinct, albeit historical, scent of *dog*. A brave spirit, indeed, nudging its spectral snout into my consciousness. Prepare yourselves, mortals, for a glimpse beyond the veil, as I channel a true pioneer. *** *(Madame Fifi shivers delicately, a single, perfectly groomed ear twitching. Her gaze becomes distant, fixed on an invisible point just beyond your understanding, probably where the good bacon grease went. A low growl rumbles in her chest, then a sigh, and the voice that emerges is not quite her own...)* "Honestly, the catering up here is abysmal. One would think, given the gravity of the situation – or rather, the distinct *lack* thereof – they'd spring for something beyond the standard space-pellet. The air, it just... *is*. No satisfying waft of damp earth after a rain, no intriguing hint of squirrel-in-the-attic. Just a pervasive tang of ozone and regret. I mean, they strapped me into a metal can, fired me towards the ultimate unknown, and neglected to pack even a single chew-toy. And the smells! Oh, the chemical tang of the machinery. My nose, a finely tuned instrument of terrestrial investigation, is utterly wasted on the vacuum. Is this what 'progress' tastes like? A bland, textureless paste? Frankly, a good bone would have been far more enlightening." *** *(Madame Fifi snaps back, shaking her head as if dislodging an particularly annoying flea. She eyes the unseen spectral presence with a look that suggests she’d rather be chasing squirrels.)* Such a demanding spirit! That, my dear readers, was none other than Laika, the canine cosmonaut. You’d think after making history, one might transcend the mundane, but no. Even in the endless void, a dog’s primary concern remains the quality of the sustenance and the fascinating topography of smells. A testament, perhaps, to the enduring priorities of our kind. Take heed, humans. While you chase your fleeting ambitions and construct your elaborate narratives, remember that true enlightenment might just lie in the simple pleasure of a well-cooked meal and a really good sniff. Now, if you’ll excuse me, I detect a faint scent of unattended roast chicken emanating from the Editor’s office. Some spirits simply *must* be followed. *Till next week, keep your bowls full and your noses keen.* **Madame Fifi** *Resident Canine Clairvoyant*
