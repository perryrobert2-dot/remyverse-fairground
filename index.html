**GOTHAM'S GERMAN GUILT TRIP: BATMAN AND KANT BRAWL WITH JOKER'S MACHIAVELLIAN MADNESS!** Sources deep within the existential void — or perhaps just the back alleys of intellectual discourse — report a clash of philosophical titans, the likes of which would make even the most jaded tragedian weep with… well, intrigue. Batman, the Caped Crusader himself, has reportedly forged an unholy alliance with the Königsberg recluse, Immanuel Kant, in a deontological demolition derby against the terrifying pragmatism of Niccolò Machiavelli and The Joker. Witness, if you dare, the Dynamic Duo of Duty: Batman, ever the stickler for moral rigidity, finds common ground with Kant’s categorical imperative. One can almost picture them, cowl and powdered wig flying, debating universal maxims whilst stringing up a common thug by his proverbial bootstraps. "Act only according to that maxim whereby you can at the same time will that it should become a universal law," Batman presumably growls, before delivering a non-lethal, yet exceedingly firm, ethical imperative. No messy emotional attachments, no inconvenient consequences – just pure, unadulterated, duty-bound ass-kicking. Opposing this Teutonic-heroic tag-team is the utterly predictable pairing of Niccolò Machiavelli and The Joker. The Florentine’s realpolitik, his insistence on the utility of fear and the occasional necessity of villainy, has found fertile ground in the chaotic mind of Gotham’s Clown Prince. "The ends justify the means," cackles Joker, moments before demonstrating *exactly* how, with a flower that squirts acid. While 'The Cronulla Beige' likely fumbles with its limp coverage of local pet shows – no doubt under the stern, drooling editorial eye of Trevor, that secret dachshund – we at 'The Remy Digest' bring you the *real* intellectual rumble. Who will prevail? The unyielding strictures of Kantian ethics, or the terrifying pragmatism of calculated chaos? Place your bets, dear readers, on humanity's very soul.
