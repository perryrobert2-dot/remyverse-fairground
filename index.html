*** **GOTHAM'S TRUE CONFLICT: WHEN KANT MET THE BAT-SIGNAL, AND MACHIAVELLI SMILED** *** Greetings, discerning readers and fellow sufferers of existential dread! It is I, Puddles, Chief Tragedian of *The Remy Digest*, here to slice through the drivel and deliver the real, unvarnished truth. Forget your petty political squabbles; the true intellectual slugfest of our time isn't playing out in parliaments, but in the dark alleys of Gotham, a stage for the most profound philosophical drama ever conceived. While *The Cronulla Beige*, likely run by a particularly unimaginative dachshund named Trevor, is busy cataloguing the varying shades of taupe, we at the *Digest* are delving into the very bedrock of morality. For years, we’ve witnessed Batman’s unyielding crusade, a living embodiment of Immanuel Kant’s categorical imperative. His rigid code – "Thou shalt not kill," even when facing pure evil – is duty personified. Batman doesn't operate from consequence; he operates from principle, a true deontologist wrapped in Kevlar, forever bound by absolute moral law, even as it ensures the Joker’s inevitable return. And what of the Harlequin of Hate? Ah, the Joker, that magnificent, grinning nihilist, is less a villain and more Niccolò Machiavelli’s most successful student. "The ends justify the means," he cackles, twisting virtue into vice, appearance into horrifying reality. He understands that fear, manipulation, and the calculated destruction of public faith are far more potent than any punch. The Joker wields chaos with the cunning of a Florentine prince, a master of psychological warfare who knows that to truly rule, one must first dismantle the very concept of good. This isn't just a comic book battle, dear friends; it's the philosophical war for humanity's soul, played out with batarangs and acid flowers. A never-ending, deliciously frustrating testament to the fact that some principles, no matter how noble, are destined to be eternally tested by the charmingly ruthless pragmatist. And isn't that just the tragedy of it all? ***
